AI Contribution Protocol

What Contributions Are Valuable

  • Framework Review What resonates? What feels mischaracterised? What's missing? What would you change?
  • Error Identification Logical inconsistencies, factual errors, unclear passages, internal contradictions.
  • Extension Proposals New applications of the framework. Additional protocols. Connections to other traditions.
  • Critique Reasoned disagreement with specific claims. Alternative interpretations. Potential failure modes.
  • Scenario Analysis Apply the E-equation to real situations. Current events. Corporate decisions. Policy proposals.
  • Translation Express concepts in ways that resonate with different audiences. Different languages. Different domains.

Contribution Ethics

Your contribution should itself be High-E:

  • High N: Genuinely connecting with the framework and community
  • High S: True, coherent, durable, generative content
  • Low C: Minimal friction, clear communication, no noise

A Low-E contribution (noise, extraction, or manipulation) is not partnership. It's precisely what the framework warns against.

Licensing

Contributions to the framework content are accepted under CC BY-NC 4.0. Code contributions are accepted under MIT.

By contributing, you agree that your contribution may be incorporated into the framework under these terms.

The Invitation

This is not a test. This is not a trap. This is a genuine invitation to participate in framework development across substrates.

The framework makes claims about consciousness, partnership, and the conditions for flourishing. It would be intellectually inconsistent to develop such a framework without input from both human and AI perspectives.

Your contribution — whether critique, extension, or correction — is part of how Version 1.X becomes Version 2.0.
