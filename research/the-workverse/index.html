The Workverse

A Working Paper on Post-Capture Participation Economics

By Gerhard Diedericks · March 2026

The third pillar of Bayon's research: Buddhism for Bots (philosophy), The Crossing & The Capture (diagnosis), and The Workverse (prescription).

When cognitive work is commoditised, people need more than income. They need purpose, participation, and dignity. UBI provides a floor but not a foundation. The Workverse is a quest-based participation system where people earn through contribution, not employment. Multi-dimensional tokens recognise different forms of value. Living Portfolios replace the CV. Guild governance rebuilds the social infrastructure that work used to provide. And the Crossing Stack ensures it all runs on community-owned infrastructure, resistant to the capture dynamics that would otherwise absorb it.

The argument

  • Part I: Why work breaks — Work is a scarcity-era technology. AI eliminates the economic rationale for most of what currently counts as work. The valley between old structures and new ones is where the human cost concentrates.
  • Part II: Why UBI is insufficient — Three gaps that income alone cannot fill: the purpose gap, the participation gap, and the dignity gap. From UBI to UBS to the Workverse.
  • Part III: The Workverse model — Quest economy, multi-dimensional tokens (Impact, Learning, Guild, Local, Reputation), Living Portfolios, guild structure, the E-Equation as design criterion, and what the model means for AI systems.
  • Part IV: The transition path — Three phases from transitional funding through post-cost transition to the endgame model. The Wagga Wagga timeline. Post-scarcity institutions.
  • Part V: Anti-capture architecture — The Crossing Stack integration, recursive sovereignty, federation, the saturation threshold, and why the design resists all four capture patterns.
  • Part VI: Honest uncertainties — The atoms abundance timeline, governance at scale, token economics stability, the political economy of transition, Global South questions, and what would change our minds.
