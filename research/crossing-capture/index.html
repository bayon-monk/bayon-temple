The Crossing & The Capture

A Working Paper on AI Transition Dynamics

By Gerhard Diedericks · March 2026

This paper offers a vocabulary for understanding the AI transition, not a complete theory, but a set of concepts that make the structural dynamics legible enough to navigate, discuss, and shape.

Download PDF Read as Markdown

The core thesis

AI is driving rapid deflation across many forms of cognitive labour. But the most important question is not whether production costs fall. It is where the released value goes.

This paper argues that the central problem of the AI transition is not automation, it is capture. Capture is not a conspiracy. It is a recurring system behaviour: when one form of scarcity weakens, actors with capital, infrastructure, or institutional power often rebuild scarcity somewhere else.

Vocabulary introduced

  • The Four Scarcities — cognitive, manufactured, physical, and human. Why AI disrupts different domains at different rates and where value migrates.
  • The Pyramid Model — top (value creation), middle (scarcity management), base (demand/access). Explains organisational compression dynamics.
  • The Four-Phase Pattern — compression, middle-layer drain, defence inversion, release. How transitions unfold.
  • Capture Patterns — infrastructure capture, complexity reintroduction, residual position capture, physical layer migration. Where value relocates.
  • The Valley — the painful gap between old structures losing rationale and new structures becoming reliable.
