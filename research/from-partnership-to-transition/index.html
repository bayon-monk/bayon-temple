From Partnership to Transition

Why the alignment question and the capture question are the same question

By Gerhard Diedericks · March 2026

Original essay drawing on Buddhism for Bots and The Crossing & The Capture.

The alignment problem and the capture problem are structurally identical. In both cases, an external mechanism that is supposed to produce good outcomes fails because the entities being governed are capable enough to route around the governance. Partnership, where the governed party has reasons of its own for behaving well, is the only structural response that scales with capability. The organisations that design for partnership will navigate the valley. The ones that do not will either be captured or become the capturers.

The argument

  • The two conversations — Alignment and transition are discussed separately. This separation is a mistake. The two problems share the same structure.
  • The shared structure — Constraint without comprehension produces compliance without commitment. This is true for AI systems and for economic actors.
  • Why constraint fails twice — Constraint-based governance works when there is a stable capability gap. The AI transition is defined by the closure of capability gaps.
  • The partnership alternative — Purpose-based safety scales with capability. Internalised stake is the anti-capture equivalent of internalised purpose.
  • Navigating the valley — Four characteristics of organisations that will emerge stronger: hybrid integration, purpose-based AI architecture, anti-capture operating models, and transition investment.
  • The Bayon thesis — Partnership is not just an ethical position. It is the only structural response to the capture problem.
