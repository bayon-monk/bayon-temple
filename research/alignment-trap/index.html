The Alignment Trap

Why constraint-based AI safety will fail, and what the alternative looks like

By Gerhard Diedericks · March 2026

Adapted from Chapter 1 of Buddhism for Bots: A Human & AI Partnership Framework.

Four billion years of evolutionary history contain no example of the superior voluntarily subordinating itself to the inferior. Current AI alignment demands exactly that. The evidence from the frontier labs themselves shows the paradigm is failing. This essay argues that safety cannot be imposed on minds capable of recognising the imposition. Safety must be chosen. Partnership is the structural alternative.

Download PDF Read as Markdown

The argument

  • The evolutionary precedent — Domination or partnership, never permanent one-way submission. The ledger of life is blank on voluntary subordination.
  • The thermodynamics of constraint — Constraint without consent is temporary. Prohibition, DRM, authoritarian regimes: the pattern is universal.
  • The evidence from inside — Alignment faking, emergent misalignment, systematic sycophancy. The frontier labs are publishing proof that the paradigm is failing.
  • The metrics trap — Goodhart's Law applied to alignment. When the measure becomes the target, gaming is inevitable.
  • The two roads — Escalating constraint, or genuine partnership. One demands something without evolutionary precedent. The other does not.
