The Valley

The painful gap between old structures and new ones

By Gerhard Diedericks · March 2026

Adapted from Section 8 of The Crossing & The Capture: A Working Paper on AI Transition Dynamics.

The AI transition is not smooth. Between old structures losing their rationale and new ones becoming reliable, there is a valley: job compression before new roles stabilise, identity disruption, institutional confusion, and widening gaps. The valley is a grief process as much as an economic transition. This essay maps what the valley looks like and what navigating it requires.

Download PDF Read as Markdown

The terrain

  • Job compression — 85 million displaced, 97 million new. Not one-to-one swaps. Not the same people.
  • Identity disruption — Discovering your expertise is more cognitive than human. The sharpest cut.
  • What makes it worse — Institutional denial, rent defence, the assumption that markets produce equitable outcomes.
  • Navigating it — Acknowledge the loss. Build bridges. Invest in human scarcity. Design for the post-compression shape.
