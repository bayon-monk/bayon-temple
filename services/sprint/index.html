The problem

Most organisations focus first on the model, the prototype, or the feature.

But agentic systems succeed or fail at the operating model level. What should the agent actually do? What should remain human-led? Where do review and escalation happen? How are errors caught and corrected? What behavioural standards apply? How do we measure whether the system is improving or drifting?

Without clear answers to those questions, agentic systems create friction faster than they create value.

What the sprint does

The Bayon AI Venture Design Sprint helps your team design the practical structure around an AI-native venture before complexity hardens.

We work with you to define the workflows, roles, protocols, measurement approach, and implementation path needed for effective human-AI collaboration.

Who it is for

This sprint is designed for:

  • teams building AI-first products
  • organisations introducing agents into internal workflows
  • regulated or risk-aware businesses needing stronger oversight
  • innovation teams moving from prototype to rollout
  • product leaders who need a clearer operating model before scaling

What we cover

Agentic opportunity and boundaries

We identify what the system should own, what humans should retain, and where delegation creates value versus unnecessary risk.

Human-AI workflow design

We map the roles, handoffs, review loops, escalation paths, and visibility needed for the system to function in practice.

Governance and protocol design

We define behavioural expectations, controls, decision boundaries, review mechanisms, and failure handling patterns.

Measurement and observability

We create a framework for assessing system quality over time, including key signals, review criteria, and practical operating metrics.

Build and rollout pathway

We translate the sprint outputs into a prototype, pilot, or production roadmap suited to your context.

Deliverables

By the end of the sprint, you receive:

  • Agentic operating model map
  • Human-AI role matrix
  • Workflow and review design
  • Governance and escalation protocol
  • Prompt and policy scaffold
  • Measurement framework
  • Implementation roadmap
